How We Think About GEO

Six truths we found in the data.

From watching 30M+ publish-to-citation data points each week — what you must know if you're serious about GEO.

01

AI doesn't search — it writes the answer.

AI search no longer returns a list of results. It composes a single answer from multiple sources. So the cited sources are the visibility.

→ Weekly tracking of which sources land in answers across 7 AI × 12 countries
02

One mention vs. core evidence — a 5× gap.

The same line gains very different visibility depending on whether it's a footnote or the main argument. A passing mention isn't enough.

→ GEO Score weights position, context, and accuracy
03

People don't ask in keywords.

SEO meant "Mercedes E-Class price." GEO means "a premium sedan that fits a family of four." A founder, PM, and designer ask about the same product in completely different ways.

→ We learn citation patterns by persona clusters
04

AI doesn't only read your site.

AI doesn't form answers from your domain alone. Reviews, communities, and press citations all count toward the verdict.

→ Weekly mention tracking across 80+ outlets + 5 major communities
05

Content AI can pull from easily looks different.

Volume is irrelevant. What decides citation is format AI can lift directly — schema, FAQs, clean sources.

→ Simulator outputs the exact +N pt gain for each upgrade
06

"Recommending" AI is over. "Buying" AI is next.

Beyond recommendations, AI now compares and executes purchases directly. Exposure shifts from awareness to action.

→ ChatGPT Operator · Perplexity Shopping already in our measurement scope

FAQ

Six things people ask most about Plurank.

How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO targeted "ranking on the first page of search." GEO targets "being cited inside answers from ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and others." As AI synthesizes answers from multiple sources, becoming a cited source matters more than ranking.

How accurate is the Pluora model?

Trained weekly on 30M+ "publish-to-citation" data points captured by 60 workers across 12 countries on 7 AI platforms. Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 8.6% — at industry-standard accuracy.

Why measure 12 countries simultaneously?

Same query yields different answers in Korean ChatGPT vs. US ChatGPT. Global brands must see all 12-country answers in parallel to act precisely.

What's different from existing solutions?

Most existing tools track either social or AI search alone, in a single market. Plurank unifies both across 12 countries × 12 channels and predicts citation probability before publishing.
